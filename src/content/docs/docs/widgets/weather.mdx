## Weather {#weather}

Right-click the Weather & Greetings widget to open its settings. From there, you can change things such as geolocation, temperature units, how Bonjourr greets you and a few other things.

![Lightly customised weather and greetings widget in Bonjourr](@assets/documentation/widgets/weather.png)

### Geolocation {#geolocation}

#### Approximate {#approximate}

IP-based geolocation, not that precise but good enough for most cases and **doesn't require any permission.**

#### Precise  {#precise}

More accurate geolocation but **requires a permission.** Some browsers (especially Safari) can be problematic with it, which is why we recommend choosing Manual instead.

#### Manual {#manual}

Choose which city you want the weather from, probably the best choice overall if you want accuracy and aren't much of a nomad.

<Aside type="note" icon="information" title="Tip for ambiguous city names">
If multiple cities share the same name, try selecting a nearby larger city with a unique identifier to ensure accurate results.
</Aside>

### Detailed weather {#detailed-weather}

"Detailed weather" allows you to easily get to a weather provider's website to get a detailed weather report. Once enabled, you can click on the weather widget to go to your chosen website.

## Greetings {#greetings}

Personalise how Bonjourr greets you throughout the day.

### Custom greeting {#custom-greeting}

Switch the greeting mode to **Custom** to define exactly how messages are displayed. Use the `$name` variable to insert your configured name dynamically.

Example: `Welcome home, $name!` → `Welcome home, John!`
